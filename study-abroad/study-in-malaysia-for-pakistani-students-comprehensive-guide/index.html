Part-time Work Opportunities in Malaysia for Pakistani Students

Students cannot work part-time during their semester in Malaysia. However, they can go for it during semester breaks for up to 20 hours per week. For legitimate reasons, you can go for part-time work but with a cash-on-hand basis. Some Malaysian universities do offer paid internships during the semester. That’s another way to earn some bucks during your stay in Malaysia.

Cost to Study in Malaysia from Pakistan

Malaysia promises the highest quality education while offering a cost-effective fee and living expenses. Moreover, all of the Malaysian universities have lower tuition fees than the UK, United States, Japan, and even Singapore. Besides, the living cost is affordable. You can easily get on-campus accommodation for only $150 per month. However, considering the other living expenses such as food, travelling, entertainment, laundry, and stationery, you need at least RM 1200 per month. The tuition cost of Malaysian universities may vary depending on their rankings and quality of education. However, you can expect the per semester cost from RM 10,000.

Study in Malaysia Without IELTS

Many universities in Malaysia offer students the option of PTE and IELTS. For some, IELTS is a must-have requirement to gain admission. For Masters, many universities accept MOI (Medium of Instruction) from Pakistani Universities. So, you won’t need to take IELTS but submit an English Proficiency Certificate from your previous university. However, for Bachelor’s and Diploma degree programs, IELTS is a must. But you can check for other English test options as well. Contact our best Education Consultants in Pakistan to get more information.

Scholarships in Malaysia for Pakistani Students

There are many universities in Malaysia which offer scholarships for Pakistani students. You can avail up to 170 scholarships in different Malaysian universities. Here are some most common types of scholarships you can avail of:

  1. Malaysia International Scholarship (MIS): Offers fully funded scholarships by the Malaysian government to talented students. Students get full tuition fees paid and a monthly stipend for living expenses. You must have a 3.5 CGPA, 3 years of experience, and an English proficiency certificate.
  2. Malaysian Technical Cooperation Program (MTCP): Assist students to study in Malaysia under the international collaboration for HR development. It covers full tuition fees and living expenses. You must have 2 years of experience, a bachelor’s degree, and a language proficiency certificate.
  3. ASEAN Scholarships for Master’s Degrees: This program is for students who want to study Master’s in Malaysia. This scholarship covers full tuition fees, stipends for living expenses, and airfare. Moreover, it covers fields of science and technology, humanities, and social sciences.
  4. University-Specific Scholarships: Many universities in Malaysia offer scholarships to talented Pakistani students. You can easily grab a 45% to 100% Scholarship from Malaysian universities like INTI and SEGI University.

Malaysia Study Visa for Pakistani Students

To study in Malaysia, you must have a Malaysia study visa. Universities help students to get visas directly from the embassy. However, the process is a little tricky. Here’s an overview of the steps to get a Malaysia study visa from Pakistan:

  1. Secure an offer letter from a recognized Malaysian university. You need to secure a letter of eligibility from the university and proof of payment of tuition fees.
  2. Apply for a Student Visa Approval Letter (eVAL) from the Malaysian embassy. You might need:
  • Completed visa application form
  • Offer letter from a Malaysian university
  • Passport size pictures
  • Copy of passport
  • Academic documents (transcripts and degrees)
  • Medical examination report (TB & HIV test)
  • Visa processing fee payment
  • Fulfill all conditions (including language requirements).
  1. The university submits your documents to Education Malaysia Global Services (EMGS) for visa approval.
  2. Pay Visa Processing Fee of RM 3900 (non-refundable) – As per EMGS or university guidelines.
  3. Receive eVAL (Electronic Visa Approval Letter) – Typically takes 4–6 weeks.
  4. You must pay at least the first semester’s tuition fee after receiving the eVAL and before you travel to Malaysia.
  • The university may issue a final offer letter or visa support letter after fee confirmation.
  • Fees range between RM 7000 to RM 12,000 for the first semester, depending on the course and institution.
  1. Travel to Malaysia – Book a flight after getting eVAL and travel clearance.
  2. Complete Medical Checkup in Malaysia – Within 7 days of arrival at an EMGS-approved clinic.
  3. Submit Passport for Student Pass Sticker – The University submits the passport to immigration for final visa endorsement.
  4. Receive Student Pass (Visa Sticker) – Valid for 1 year, renewable annually.

FAQs

What is the Malaysia visa price?

The Malaysia visa price in Pakistan is RM 3,900. This amount is non-refundable.

How much does it cost to study in Malaysia?

The tuition fee varies according to the universities and the courses you choose. However, you can expect it from RM 10,000 to RM 45,000. Moreover, the living costs in the UK can range from RM 800 to RM 1,200.

Can we do a part-time job in Malaysia?

No, Malaysia does not allow for direct part-time jobs during the semester. However, you can work for 20 hours/week during your semester break. Besides, some universities also offer internship programs. So you can earn and get relevant field experience.

Does Malaysia offer PSW after studying there?

No, Malaysia does not offer any post-study work visa for international students. However, you can seek dual degree study programs or transfer programs to start your degree in Malaysia and then transfer to the UK, Australia, and the USA.
