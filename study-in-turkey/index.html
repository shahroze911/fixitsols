Study in Turkey with EduCrest Migration

Are you considering studying abroad? If so, studying in Turkey is an excellent option, especially for Pakistani students. With its unique blend of history, culture, and educational excellence, Turkey has emerged as a leading destination for international students. With a 90% Muslim population, Turkey offers a welcoming environment, complete with nationwide availability of halal food. Over 200 Turkish universities provide admissions in diverse programs, including Medicine, Engineering, Information Technology, and Computer Sciences. Both partially and fully funded scholarships are available, primarily merit-based, though they come with strong competition. Moreover, part-time work is also allowed.


Benefits of Studying in Turkey

Low Tuition Fees and Affordable Living Costs

Turkey offers an excellent opportunity to pursue higher education at a low cost. Annual tuition fees at public universities range between $2,000 and $7,000, which is significantly more affordable than Western countries. Additionally, living expenses, including food, accommodation, and transportation, are budget-friendly. Their average monthly cost falls between €400 and €650. Moreover, students can work part-time for up to 24 hours per week after completing their year.

Quality Education with Global Recognition

Turkey is home to globally recognized institutions such as Ankara University, Middle East Technical University (METU), and Istanbul University, known for delivering high-quality education. Many Turkish universities offer English-taught programs, ensuring students can study without language barriers. Furthermore, Turkey provides numerous partially and fully funded scholarships specifically designed for international students, making quality education more accessible and affordable.

Strategic Location and Cultural Familiarity

Turkey’s unique geographical position, bridging Europe and Asia, makes it a gateway to both regions. Pakistani students can easily explore opportunities for internships, jobs, or networking across Europe and the Middle East. Cultural familiarity also plays a significant role in helping Pakistani students settle comfortably in Turkey. With both nations sharing a predominantly Muslim culture, the availability of halal food, religious practices, and customs ensures a welcoming environment.

Surprising Facts About Turkey for Students

Every year, over 220,000 international students choose to study in Turkey, attracted by its world-class education, rich cultural heritage, and strategic location. Turkey is home to 183 higher education institutions, including 109 state universities and 74 private non-profit institutions, providing a wide range of academic programs for students. Notably, two Turkish universities were ranked in the 2022 QS World University Rankings within the top 600, highlighting the country’s commitment to high-quality education.

Turkey offers a unique blend of Eastern and Western educational traditions, making it an ideal destination for students seeking diverse academic experiences. Aside from academics, students can enjoy the country’s historical sites, including two of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World—the Temple of Artemis and the Mausoleum at Halicarnassus. Whether you’re pursuing a Bachelor’s or Master’s degree, studying in Turkey means you can balance your academic life with the opportunity to explore historical landmarks like the Archaeological Site of Troy or the Rock Sites of Cappadocia.

Admission Requirements to Study in Turkey

To apply for your studies in Turkey, you will need the following documents:

1.      National Identity Card:  A valid Pakistani National ID card for identification

2.      Valid Passport: Your passport must be valid for at least 90 days after your planned arrival in Turkey.

3.      Attested Academic Transcripts: Official transcripts from your previous academic institutions, attested as required.

4.      Updated CV or Resume: A recent CV detailing your academic and professional background.

5.      English Proficiency Certificate: IELTS, TOEFL, or another certificate of English proficiency, as per university requirements.

6.      University Scholarship Letter: If applying for a scholarship, you need a letter confirming it.

7.      2 Recommendation Letters: Letters from academic or professional referees supporting your application.

8.      Statement of Purpose: A personal statement explaining your academic and career goals.

9.      Application Fee Receipt (35-100 USD): Proof of payment of the application fee.

Additional Notes

  • • Accommodation: Rent can vary depending on the city. Major cities like Istanbul and Ankara may have higher rental costs, but they also offer more student accommodation options.
  • • Part-Time Work: International students can work part-time (24 hours/week), helping to cover living expenses while studying in Turkey.
  • • Health Insurance: It is mandatory for international students to have health insurance in Turkey. This can be arranged either through Turkish social security or private providers.

Part-time Working Hours and After Study Work Permit

Pakistani students studying in Turkey can work part-time while pursuing their education. They can work for up to 24 hours per week during the academic year. However, it is important to obtain a work permit from the relevant authorities before starting any employment. This allows students to gain valuable work experience while studying, helping them financially and professionally.

Additionally, after completing their education in Turkey, students have the opportunity to apply for an after-study work permit. This permit allows graduates to stay in Turkey for up to one year after graduation, providing time to search for full-time employment or pursue further studies. On average, part-time students in Turkey earn around 500-700 USD per month, which helps cover living expenses and enriches their overall experience.
