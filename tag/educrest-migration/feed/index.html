Step-by-Step Admission Process for Pakistani Students

The process to study in Malaysia from Pakistan is straightforward if you follow the correct steps. Here is a complete breakdown:

Step 1 – Research and Shortlist Programs

Start by identifying the program and university that match your academic background and career goals. Compare tuition fees, program duration, language of instruction, and accreditation status. Always verify that the degree is recognized by the relevant Pakistani professional body if you plan to return and work in a regulated profession.

Step 2 – Check Entry Requirements

Most Malaysian universities require a minimum of 45â€“60% marks in FSc, A-Levels, or an equivalent qualification for undergraduate admissions. For postgraduate programs, a 16-year education (bachelor’s degree) is required. English language proficiency tests like IELTS (5.5â€“6.5 band) or TOEFL are required unless your prior medium of instruction was English. Some universities conduct their own English placement tests.

Step 3 – Submit Your Application

Applications are typically submitted online through the university’s official portal. You will need to upload scanned copies of your academic transcripts and certificates, a valid Pakistani passport, passport-sized photographs, a personal statement or statement of purpose, recommendation letters (for postgraduate programs), and an English proficiency test score if applicable.

Most Malaysian universities offer rolling admissions, meaning you can apply at any time of the year. However, major intakes are in September and February or March.

Step 4 – Receive Your Offer Letter and Pay the Acceptance Fee

Once your application is approved, you will receive a conditional or unconditional offer letter. After accepting the offer and paying the required Visa Fee or registration fee (usually USD 1000), the university will begin processing your student pass through EMGS (Education Malaysia Global Services).

Step 5 – EMGS Approval and Student Pass (Malaysia Student Visa from Pakistan)

This is one of the most critical stages. The university submits your documents to EMGS on your behalf. EMGS is the body authorized by the Malaysian Ministry of Higher Education to process student pass applications for international students. The EMGS process typically takes 4 to 8 weeks. Documents required include your offer letter, passport copy, academic transcripts, medical examination report from an EMGS-approved clinic, and passport-sized photos.

Once EMGS approves your application and issues a visa approval letter (VAL), you can travel to Malaysia and collect your student pass at the Malaysian Immigration Department upon arrival.

Step 6 – Pre-Departure Preparation

Before you fly out, arrange your accommodation (on-campus or off-campus), purchase a return or onward ticket, get a comprehensive health insurance plan, notify your bank about international transactions, and gather certified copies of all your documents. Connecting with your university’s international student office and joining Pakistani student associations in Malaysia before arrival can ease your transition significantly.
