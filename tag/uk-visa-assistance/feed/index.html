            How Much Does Study Cost in the UK?

            The expenses to study in the UK depend upon the course, university, and campus you choose.             Undergraduate

            The undergraduate tuition fee for international students, such as those from Pakistan, can range from £11000 to £38,000. Usually, the undergraduate degrees are three years long; however, they can vary in some regions, such as Scotland.

            Postgraduate

            The postgraduate tuition fee for international students can range from £9000 to £30,000. In the UK, you can complete your master’s degree in one year and get a 2-year post-study work visa.

            Can Students Work in the UK on a Student Visa?

            International students are allowed to work 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during holidays. Thus, they can easily afford their living expenses in the UK. Moreover, some universities may also offer on-campus work placements. Some of them also have attached companies that offer jobs. So, it depends upon the student’s capabilities and how much he/she can earn. Generally, students earn up to £1200 to £1500 per month.

            Graduate VISA

            International students can stay in the UK even after completing their degree on a post-study work visa. They can remain in the UK for two years and work there. However, you need to apply for this visa while living in the university before clearing out. The visa application fee is £822, and you might need to pay an immigration health surcharge (IHS Fee) of £624 for each subsequent year.

            Currency

            To manage living expenses in the UK, you need cash in your bank account and hand. In the UK, you must make transactions and pay bills in pounds and pence.             Bank Accounts

            You can open a student or temporary bank account while living in the UK. To open a bank account in the UK, you need to fulfil the following requirements:

            • Valid Passport.
            • Document from the university confirming that you are currently studying there.
            • Proof of address in Pakistan and UK
            • Bank statement from Pakistan (your personal bank account)

            You can contact your home country’s bank account to make the process easier. They might have a partnership or link with a UK-based bank. This way, you can easily create a bank account in the UK. To access the money in your UK bank account, you can:

            1. Use online banking systems such as mobile applications
            2. Visit the relevant branch of the bank
            3. Withdraw money using an ATM
            4. Ask for cash-back while making purchases through your card

Note that you can’t apply with cash and bank transfers unless you are a resident of North Korea.

                    What’s the Procedure for an IHS Fee Refund?

                    You will get the full IHS fee refund in case:

                    • Suppose you have paid the IHS fee twice or more for the same Visa. The extra amount will be refunded after the Visa is granted.
                    • If you get a visa refusal or withdraw your visa application before its decision, you will get a refund.

                    You will get the partial IHS fee refund if your visa application is submitted, but:

                    • You got a shorter visa than you filed for. For example, you asked for two years and got a Visa for one and a half years.
                    • If any dependents are refused a visa, they will get their IHS fee back.
                    • You are applying for a UK Visa again and have already paid the IHS for that period.

                    Moreover, if you have paid the IHS fee twice for the same period of 6 or more than 6 months, the partial refund amount will be rounded to the nearest 6 months. For example, if you paid for 20 months, the refund will be 18 months of IHS. Paying twice for less than 6 months of IHS is not entitled to any refund.

                    How Will I Get the Refund, and How long will it Take?

                    You don’t need to take any action if you are entitled to a partial or full IHS refund. The amount will be returned to the account you paid the IHS Fee with. You will usually get the refund within 28 days. However, if you appeal the visa application refusal and ask for a review, the refund can take longer. If your appeal is successful, you will have to pay the IHS again in case it’s refunded.

                    In What Case Do You Won’t Get a Refund?

                    You won’t get any refund if you

                    • Withdraw your visa application but have already received the Visa.
                    • You got your VISA but decided not to go to the UK.
                    • You left the UK before the ending period mentioned in the Visa.
                    • You are asked to leave the UK for any reason or mishap.
                    • You are switching to another Visa type or applying for indefinite leave to remain.

                    When Can You Start Using NHS with Immigration Health Surcharge? 

                    You can start using the NHS free of cost from the starting date of your Visa. However, IHS payment is a must. If you are in the UK and the Home Office hasn’t decided on your VISA approval, you might need to pay for NHS. Moreover, you might need to pay for services like prescriptions, dental treatment, and eye care. Besides, to access NHS, you may need to show the BRP (biometric residence permit) or a share code in case of digital immigration status.

                    • Valid Passport: Applicants must possess a valid passport with at least six months’ validity beyond the intended duration of stay in the UK.
                    • Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS): Obtaining a CAS from a UK educational institution is mandatory. The CAS serves as proof of acceptance onto a course of study and contains essential details such as the course duration, fees, and sponsorship information.
                    • Proof of English Proficiency: Most UK universities require international students to demonstrate proficiency in English through standardized tests like the IELTS, PTE, ELLT, TOEFL or Language Cert Academic Selt.
